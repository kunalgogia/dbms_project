Our in-patient department houses over 3000 animals at any given point. These include dogs, cats, cattle, monkeys,
rabbits, birds, goats, sheep, camels, donkeys, horses and more. They are further divided into large and
small animal departments. Each one has a separate ICU and maternity section

Dogs are further segregated based on the nature of their illness. The cattle are sub-divided into cows and buffaloes.
The birds into birds of prey, other birds, water birds, small birds.

They receive treatment daily, and cases brought by ISHVHCC's ambulance service are also treated and homed in the IPD.
People who cannot afford to pay treatment charges for animals are receive free treatment and temporary hospitalisation
for their animals in the IPD.

ISVHCC endeavours to provide a balanced, nutritious diet for all its animals with a healthy mix of carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins
and minerals. The cattle get straw and green fodder mixed with choker, churi, gur and salt licks.
The cats and dogs receive dalia, bread, milk, rusk, occasionally eggs, or meat received from hotel leftovers, vegetables, rice, bread,
soyabean. Rabbits and birds get fresh fruit and vegetables, monkeys get rotis and fruit while ducks and peacocks get grain and fresh greens.
